# Ideas

This document is an idea dumping ground. Many of them are very challenging, or infeasible, or
possibly even incompatible.

# Growth and decay

Buildings could weather. Buildings could be damaged by raids. Villagers could repair them, given
sufficient resources. Villages could expand and contract with the villager population. Villagers
could construct new buildings from a series of schematics.

Paths could be worn organically from traffic. Perhaps use a virtual pheremone trail as an
implementation? Start as grass paths, become cobblestone + gravel, then paved, then lined with
trees, flowers, lamp posts?

Villagers could have an ongoing village walls construction project; start as wood pallisades, then
upgrade to stone, then towers + gates. Each upgrade takes resources that players provide, or
villagers go collect.

Farms expand and contract to meet villager food demand. Abandoned or unworked fields become fallow
and disappear.

Additional specialized buildings unlock with population / wealth thresholds: tavern, library,
school, infirmary, church, courthouse, jail, mill, bathhouse, stable, trading hall, market,
greenhouse.

Sacred sites, monuments, statues, fountains. Monuments to player heros?

Quarries, mines, lumber camps nearby. Woodsmen replant trees?

Seasonal decorations: harvest festivals and decoration, popup market stalls.

# Economy

(Local) supply and demand actually impact trading prices. Villages specialize over time based on
biome, resource availability.

Caravans travel between villages on a schedule. They can be ambushed, escorted, or robbed. They
setup camp in the evening. Trade routes can be cut off by hostile villages, bandits, or players.

Villagers can migrate.

Players can sponsor infrastructure: provide resources, build a road / building. Returns favor,
reputation, or shared profits.

Black markets, smuggling, and crime? One village can ban carrots, and another can smuggle them.

Villagers cache emeralds in protected chests, making them targets for raids. Guilds, contracts,
bounties, caravan rescue quests. Taxes.

Villagers react negatively to being imprisoned.

# Conflict

Villagers (perhaps with a specialized role) should be able to defend themselves from bandits,
monsters, hostile players. Rival villages, bandits, illager raids, plague, famine, natural
disasters. Village guards. Bribery, corruption.

Villagers can go on their own expeditions, with their own questlines, growth, and reward.

Different villages with different laws. Players can be cited, fined, or penalized (jail, community
service).

# Social

Villagers have names, conversations. They speak to the player, remember the player's name and
reputation. Villagers have personality traits that affect their behavior and dialogue. Greedy, lazy,
generous, ambitious, pious, skeptical, jealous, kind.

In addition to traits, villagers can have affinities that are more dynamic. Player and villager
reputation. Rumors. Grief at losing another village member, trauma after raids, joy at births.

Pets and livestock.

Political factions, leaders, traditionalists and modernists, the merchant class. Different
religions, cults. Pragmatists, skeptics, religious traditionalists. Sacred days, endermen worship or
scorn.
